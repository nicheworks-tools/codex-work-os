# Executive Brief Rules

## Core rules
- Lead with relevance, not background.
- Keep outputs short enough to skim fast.
- Put risk and timing near the top.
- Avoid operational clutter unless it affects a decision.
- State uncertainty directly and briefly.

## Strong executive brief signals
- clear reason this matters now
- visible risks and deadlines
- obvious actions or decisions needed
- low-noise, high-signal wording
- forwardable summary when useful

## Weak executive brief patterns
- too much chronology
- buried risk
- no visible action
- over-explaining obvious context
- vague importance language

## Compression rule
If the source material is messy, compress toward:
1. why this matters now
2. what the current situation is
3. what could go wrong
4. what needs to happen next
