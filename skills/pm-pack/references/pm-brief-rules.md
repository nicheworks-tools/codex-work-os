# PM Brief Rules

## Core rules
- Prefer clarity over completeness.
- Prefer current reality over optimistic framing.
- Put blockers before fine detail.
- Distinguish shipped, in progress, and at risk.
- If a decision is missing, say so directly.

## Good PM brief signals
- clear reporting window
- visible movement
- explicit blocker ownership
- real next actions
- readable by non-followers

## Weak PM brief patterns
- vague "making progress" language
- no owner for blockers
- too much chronology, not enough synthesis
- hidden risks
- missing decision points

## Compression rule
If the source material is messy, compress toward:
1. what changed
2. what is stuck
3. what needs a decision
4. what happens next
