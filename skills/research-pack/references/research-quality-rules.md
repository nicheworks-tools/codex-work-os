# Research Quality Rules

## Core rules
- Always make the decision question explicit.
- Evidence comes before recommendation.
- Separate observed facts from interpretation.
- Keep trade-offs visible.
- State uncertainty without hedging the whole memo into uselessness.

## Strong research memo signals
- clear question
- explicit criteria
- visible comparison
- recommendation tied to evidence
- acknowledged uncertainty

## Weak research memo patterns
- conclusion first, evidence weak
- source dump without synthesis
- hidden assumptions
- vague "pros and cons" without implications
- recommendation without trade-off discussion

## Compression rule
If the source material is messy, compress toward:
1. what is being decided
2. what the evidence says
3. where the trade-offs are
4. what should happen next
