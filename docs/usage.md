# Usage Guide

## How to think about these packs

Each pack is a domain-level skill.
Use the pack that best matches the dominant job to be done.

Examples:

- use `research-pack` for comparisons, synthesis, and decision support
- use `pm-pack` for status and coordination work
- use `sales-pack` for lead triage and reply drafting
- use `exec-assist-pack` for meeting prep and executive summaries
- use `cs-pack` for support-case summarization and escalation prep

---

## Recommended prompting style

You do not need rigid invocation syntax.
Natural requests are fine as long as the intent is clear.

Helpful requests tend to include:

- the source material
- the target audience
- the target format
- the tone or constraints
- whether a ready-to-send draft is needed

---

## Good requests

- “Turn these updates into a short PM weekly brief.”
- “Compare these three options and draft a decision memo.”
- “Draft a polite customer reply based on this thread.”
- “Prepare a meeting brief for an executive who has not seen this before.”

## Weak requests

- “Make this better.”
- “Write something useful.”
- “Summarize maybe?”

---

## Human review expectations

The outputs are designed to be highly usable, but they are still drafts or structured syntheses.
Review matters especially when:

- facts are incomplete
- internal context is missing
- the output will be sent externally
- the matter is sensitive, legal, financial, or reputational
